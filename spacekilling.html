About the Game

Space Killing is a tactical tabletop RPG set in a hostile universe where survival is never guaranteed. Built on a d6 system designed for grid-based combat, it emphasizes tactical positioning, squad coordination, and the psychological toll of violence. Characters don't level up — they learn from what they survive.

The game deliberately strips away social and mental stats. In a universe this brutal, being dumb is a death sentence, and personality should be roleplayed, not rolled. What remains is a tight system focused on physical capabilities, knowledge acquisition, and staying alive long enough to matter.

Core Systems

Tactical Phase Combat
Structured rounds with Maintenance → Move → Action → Melee → Ending phases. No initiative rolls — sides alternate, forcing real tactical decisions.
Lethal by Design
HP equals Body stat (2-4 for humans). Critical hits cause mutilation. You have 5 rounds to be saved at 0 HP. Combat has consequences.
Organic Progression
No character levels. XP is rolled as dice (4+ to confirm gain). You can only improve skills you actually used. Want to learn driving? Find a teacher and drive.
Psychology System
Anguish accumulates. Failed Willpower saves fill your instability track. At 10 slots, roll for disorders — anxiety, PTSD, schizophrenia. Mental health matters mechanically.

Character Classes

Six starting professions across three archetypes — Military, Scientific, and Pathfinder. Each defines your initial knowledge and gear, but progression is unrestricted. Specialization keeps you alive early; adaptation keeps you alive later.

Gear & World

Every piece of equipment is designed with the system in mind — weight limits matter, armor plates break, weapons jam. The crafting system uses blueprints and material quality tiers. Master-crafted items require skill and luck.
