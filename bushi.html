Bushi: L'Enigma del Samurai

Role Experience Designer
Client Museu de la Garrotxa, Olot
Type Immersive RPG Event

The Event

Bushi: L'Enigma del Samurai was a live-action puzzle experience designed for the Museu de la Garrotxa in Olot. The event was created for the inauguration of the "Cambra de Meravelles" (Cabinet of Curiosities) exhibition, with the goal of attracting younger audiences to the museum.

Small groups of participants entered the museum at night and were transported into a fantastical world where a samurai asked for their help. The mission: find his lost katana. To succeed, players had to interact with costumed characters representing figures from different historical periods — a Carlist soldier, a Viking, a medieval monk — each connected to artifacts displayed in the exhibition.

Design Elements

Narrative Framework
A samurai's lost katana becomes the central mystery, giving players a clear goal that ties together the museum's diverse collection.
Character Actors
Costumed performers played historical figures — each linked to exhibition pieces, creating personal connections to the artifacts.
Puzzle Chain
Groups solved interconnected enigmas, with each character providing clues that led to the next, building toward the final reveal.
Night Setting
Running the event after hours transformed the museum space, creating atmosphere and exclusivity that enhanced immersion.

The Samurai

The samurai armor from the Cambra de Meravelles exhibition — the central character of the experience

Event Documentation

Outcome

The event successfully engaged younger visitors with museum content through play. By turning passive observation into active participation, players formed memorable connections with historical artifacts they might otherwise have walked past. This was the first of several escape room-style experiences I designed for cultural institutions.
