Role Industrial Designer
Tools AutoCAD, Blender, TopSolid

Project Overview

A furniture design project exploring the intersection of traditional joinery and modern manufacturing techniques. The VRGS Chair features a reclining backrest mechanism and modular construction, designed for both comfort and ease of fabrication.

The design process moved from initial hand sketches through detailed technical drawings in AutoCAD, with component modeling in TopSolid for precision manufacturing specifications. The final prototype validates the mechanical reclining system and demonstrates the feasibility of the modular assembly approach.

Concept Development

The design began with hand sketches exploring the fundamental challenge: creating a reclining mechanism that would be both mechanically sound and visually integrated with the chair's structural frame. Early iterations tested different pivot points and back angles.

First concept sketch — establishing proportions and basic geometry

Technical Documentation

Detailed AutoCAD plans provide orthographic views and mechanical specifications. The drawings include the reclining mechanism geometry (108° angle, 56.02 cm diagonal when reclined) and standardized component dimensions for production.

Orthographic projections at 1:10 scale showing front, side, and top views with dimensional annotations

Component Design

The chair is composed of nine distinct components (A1-A9), each designed for straightforward fabrication and assembly. The modular approach allows for material substitution and simplifies manufacturing workflows.

Complete parts breakdown with isometric assembly view — all components shown at 1:10 scale

3D Modeling

Individual components were modeled in TopSolid for precision manufacturing. The parametric approach ensures consistency across parts and enables rapid iteration on critical dimensions like seat depth and backrest angle.

Design Reflection

This project reinforced the importance of moving fluidly between analog sketching and digital precision tools. The hand sketches captured design intent quickly, while AutoCAD forced resolution of dimensional conflicts early. TopSolid's parametric modeling proved essential for ensuring component compatibility.

The reclining mechanism presented the core challenge — balancing structural stability with user comfort. The final 108° angle offers a comfortable recline while keeping the pivot geometry simple enough for reliable fabrication. Future iterations could explore upholstered seating surfaces and refined joinery details.
