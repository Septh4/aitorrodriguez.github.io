Sants Escape Room

Role Designer & Writer
Client Museu dels Sants, Olot
Participants 500+ Players

The Experience

An escape room experience designed for the Museu dels Sants in Olot — a museum dedicated to the traditional religious statue workshops that made the town famous. Players were guided by "Giuliano," a mysterious Italian character with ties to the Chicago Outfit, who challenged them to find eight missing Busts of Limpias and solve their enigmas before time ran out.

The experience combined physical exploration of the museum with a custom-built app that delivered video clues and tracked progress. Groups of up to 7 players (or two competing teams of 7) navigated through 9 puzzle rooms, each connected to specific artifacts and the museum's history.

The App

To guide players through the experience, I developed a companion app. Each room was represented by a different Bust of Limpias — tapping on one would play a video of Giuliano delivering clues in his signature mix of Catalan and Italian. The app replaced the need for printed materials and allowed for timed competitive play between two teams.

The Story

Giuliano is an aging member of the Chicago Outfit who has been visiting the Olot workshops for decades. His "gifts" to associates back home — increasingly large Busts of Limpias — aren't gifts at all. They're death sentences. The larger the bust, the more final the message.

Players piece together his history through the puzzles, discovering the dark purpose behind his orders while racing against the clock. The final revelation ties together the museum's craft tradition with a noir crime narrative.

"Aquests busts de Límpias no són simples regali. Els meus amics de Chicago no són simples amics, més aviat al contrari: som gent despiadada, sense escrúpols. Cada bust de Límpias, una advertència. I com més gran és el bust, més inútil són els plors i les lamentacions."
— Giuliano, Final Monologue

Outcome

The escape room ran successfully with over 500 participants, transforming how visitors engaged with the museum's collection. By wrapping historical artifacts in a compelling narrative, players developed genuine curiosity about the craftsmanship traditions they were exploring — turning passive observation into active investigation.
